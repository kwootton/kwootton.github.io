Kate Wootton

I am an interdisciplinary ecologist interested in the mechanisms that drive the structure and dynamics of complex networks, predominantly food webs (the network of who-eats-whom in an ecological community). I integrate empirical and computational approaches to try and understand why networks have the structure they do, what the consequences of that structure are, and what that may mean for the future.

I am currently a post-doctoral researcher in the BioFrontiers Intstitue at the University of Colorado, Boulder, working with Aaron Clauset and Daniel Larremore. There I am studying the academic ecosystem, to learn how the traits of academic fields, institutions, and researchers shape academic networks.
